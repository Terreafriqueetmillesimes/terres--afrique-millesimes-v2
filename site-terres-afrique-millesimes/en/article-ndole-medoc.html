N'dolè & Haut-Médoc

The founding pairing of House TAM, decoded step by step.

By Marie Louise Ticki · Prestige Sommelier

There are pairings that cannot simply be explained: they must be felt. The marriage of Cameroonian n'dolè and a great Haut-Médoc is one of them. It is the founding pairing of our House, the one through which we love to present our craft: bringing France and Africa into dialogue through wine.

Understanding the dish

N'dolè is an emblematic dish of the Cameroonian coast. Its bitter leaves, blanched at length, are seasoned with ground peanuts, prawns and smoked meat. The result: a noble bitterness, a rich unctuousness and a smoky depth that call for a wine of character.

Why Haut-Médoc

A Haut-Médoc dominated by Cabernet Sauvignon brings exactly what the dish demands: a firm tannic backbone that answers the richness of the peanut, notes of cedar and liquorice that extend the smoked meat, and a freshness that cleanses the palate between mouthfuls.

The bitterness of the dish and the tannin of the wine do not clash: they recognise each other.

Service, step by step

Serve the wine at 17 °C. Decant it one hour before the meal to soften the tannins. Choose a wide glass that allows the bouquet to breathe. And above all, serve the dish piping hot: the heat releases the smokiness that will forge the link.

The TAM signature pairing

Château Lestage Simon, Cuvée Tradition 2019 — AOC Haut-Médoc, Cru Bourgeois.

« The pot of friendship simmers slowly, but it feeds the whole family. » — Cameroonian proverb

Three more pairings to explore

Poulet DG & Burgundy — the richness of this celebratory dish, its slow-cooked vegetables and plantains, finds in the silk of a Pinot Noir from the Côte de Nuits a partner of the greatest elegance.

Pairing

Domaine Jean Bouchard, Gevrey-Chambertin Vieilles Vignes 2020.

« The traveller's chicken is never eaten alone. » — Bamiléké proverb

Thiéboudienne & Champagne — set against the Senegalese fish rice, its tomato and vegetables, the tension and effervescence of a brut deliver a freshness that revives every mouthful.

Pairing

Maison J. Charpentier, Cuvée Réserve Brut.

« The hand that shares the rice never forgets the guest. » — Wolof proverb

Kedjenou & Loire white — this Ivorian dish slow-cooked in a sealed pot, fragrant and tender, flourishes alongside a dry Chenin, vivid and mineral, whose freshness highlights the spices.

Pairing

Loire Valley — dry Chenin selection available on request.

« Peppers that ripen together are shared together. » — Akan proverb

In conclusion

A great pairing is not a rule: it is an encounter. Our role, at Terres d'Afrique & Millésimes, is to bring about these encounters with rigour and generosity — one glass, one dish, and the story that unites them.
