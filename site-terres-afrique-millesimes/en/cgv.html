Terms & Conditions

1. Pricing

Prices are displayed in euros and, for export, in FCFA. Fixed exchange rate: 1 € = 655.957 FCFA. Export prices are quoted exclusive of French taxes — export regime, VAT exempt under article 262 of the French General Tax Code (CGI).

2. Temperature-controlled delivery

Packaging in isothermal cases, transport by refrigerated vehicle maintained at a constant 15 °C. Unified rate: 0.99 € ex-VAT per bottle, invoiced in addition to the order. Average lead time: 2 to 5 working days in mainland France. European Union and Corsica: on quotation. Breakage rate with partner VITICOLIS below 0.15% — transport insurance included.

3. Payment

Private clients: payment at time of order by credit card (Stripe / PayPal). HoReCa: 30 days end of month subject to agreement. Africa Export: 100% upfront by SWIFT transfer.

4. Africa export conditions

Minimum quantity: 10 mixed cases (minimum 60 bottles). Incoterm EXW Versailles by default, other incoterms available on request. Countries covered: Cameroon, Ivory Coast, Senegal, Gabon. Delivery lead time: 4 to 8 weeks depending on destination and customs formalities.

5. Customs & local taxes

Customs duties, import taxes and formalities are the exclusive responsibility of the client at destination. Export shipping costs are also the responsibility of the client.

6. Retention of title

Goods remain the property of Terre Afrique et Millésimes until full payment of the purchase price.

7. Storage

Beyond 10 days after goods are made available for collection, storage charges of 25 € ex-VAT per week per pallet may be applied.

8. Responsible Drinking & Sale to Minors (French Évin Law)

Excessive alcohol consumption is harmful to health. Please drink responsibly. The consumption of alcoholic beverages during pregnancy, even in small quantities, may have serious consequences for the child's health.

The sale of alcohol to minors is strictly prohibited. By placing an order, the purchaser declares they are 18 years of age or older.

9. Applicable law & jurisdiction

These terms and conditions are governed by French law. Any dispute falls under the jurisdiction of the courts of Versailles.
